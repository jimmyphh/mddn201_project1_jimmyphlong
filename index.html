Intro: Concept

wow, is that an i7 processor? It must be good!

The main focus here is desktop computers(PC), side note, PC is the acronym for ‘personal computer’ for those who are unsure. Modern day personal computers can vary from touch screen phones, drawing tablets to desktop PCs. Most people will have a PC of some sort however when it comes to purchasing the right ones and how to go by it. This is the part where it can get confusing for those who are inexperienced with computers. From my personal experience, many people I know are not very aware of how expensive retail desktop computers are compared to building one yourself. It can be deceiving if you are inexperienced. YouTuber, BitWit , goes into more depth here. Prebuilt computer systems tend to be overpriced and advertised to appear to be the best option which is usually never the case, though it is stress-free, since you buy, leave and plug it in and it will turn on.

Some people do not realise how much they spent on an electronic product until they use it. Did they buy a computer too expensive with not enough processing power? Did they buy something too expensive with an unnecessary amount of processing power? These are the questions that concern me, who is always on a budget, so I am very selective with what I want to pay for. After many hours of research, building PCs, purchasing computers and trialing and error, I want to share my experience and knowledge of PC building. This link will provide a good idea of what was mentioned: Should you build a PC?

This project is inspired by my hobby of PC gaming and building computers. The first goal of this project is to make this useful for everyone. Friends, family or even people of the internet with information on the begin-to-end process of building a computer, in hope of encouraging and inspiring others. To be more specific, this project will help people decide whether or not buying a retail desktop PC is worth more than purchasing individual parts to create your own. Overall, it can be challenging, enjoying but rewarding, since you get it done and usually it is cheaper. On the flip side, buying a prebuilt system is more generally reliable and easier since you are buying a functionable PC. So it is not all bad.

Having your own desktop computer is very possible as oppose to many, many, many years ago when computers were ridiculously expensive. With a few budgeting tips and tricks, a desktop is available for everyone.
